3DS Modding



What is 3ds modding?


3ds modding is the process of making the 3ds behave differently than what the people intended for it to do. This opens up a world of possibilities for other mods. The steps are simple, but if you don't know what most of these terms mean, you'll be lost, causing it to take around an hour of your time

Step 3: Important facts

To go to the root of your SD card means that you have to go to the far back of the SD card.

The size of the SD card would depend on the model of console. A "New" series console can run at max a 32GB SDXHC card. An older 3DS card uses a 32GB SD Card (Larger one. There are containers that you can buy at walmart for around $12 that come with 2 SDXHC cards and a SDXHC to SD card reader.)

A modded 3DS for starters can run extra or different files than a non-modded 3DS, that can't run any files that are not game save data or officially added to the SD card through legal means. Another difference is that modded consoles have the capability to bring back online play to games that were shut down, while non-modded consoles lost all online support almost 2 years ago. The last difference is the performance statistics. The modded consoles can run games with better statistics and with other add-ons while non-modded 3DS consoles can't even run their own games at decent statistics.
